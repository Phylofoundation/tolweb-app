More information about treehouse contribution categories

Building treehouses offers you the opportunity to publish work that is related to the study of organisms and biodiversity. Though you must initially select one type of treehouse contribution, you will be able to assign multiple treehouse types to your work once you are in the treehouse data entry page. This means that if you have an investigation that you would also like to call a story, just choose one of the categories to start off. Then, in the Treehouse Type section of the treehouse data entry page, you can check off the other treehouse categories that you would like to call your work.

Below is a brief explanation of each category. For more information and ideas see Fun Treehouse Building Activities.

Investigation: Choose an investigation to publish a

  • Science experiment or project
  • Field study
  • Scientific investigation or research study
  • Science journal entry
  • Scientific observation of an organism
  • return to compose a new treehouse contribution

Story: Publish your story! We are looking for

  • Nonfiction and fictional stories, from accounts of neighborhood wildlife to tales of life as a lion
  • Stories about your experiences exploring organisms in the field, classroom or lab
  • Picture books
  • News stories and interviews
  • return to compose a new treehouse contribution

Fun and Games: Let's play! Some example of games we would like you to contribute are

  • Computer games
  • Playground games
  • Card games
  • Trivia games
  • Game cards
  • return to compose a new treehouse contribution

Art and Culture: Some examples of art and culture contributions we welcome are

  • Instructions on how to create organism related arts and crafts projects
  • Poems and songs
  • Folk remedies for various ailments, recipes for the kitchen
  • Descriptions of economic and cultural use of organisms (such as corn, reindeer, scarab beetles, silk worms)
  • A multimedia gallery or "art exhibit"
  • return to compose a new treehouse contribution

Teacher Resources: Add materials to guide teaching and learning such as

  • Units, lessons and activities
  • Assessments
  • Glossary word lists
  • Materials to guide student exploration
  • Print, media and internet resources
  • return to compose a new treehouse contribution

WebQuests: Create an interactive activity for

  • Exploring and learning about a topic
  • Guiding others through web resources
  • Helping learners use information for their own purposes

More information about treehouse portfolios

Choosing to create a portfolio offers the opportunity to present different ToL pages as a group of complementary materials. Because teacher resources often need to collect several pages in order to teach a concept, by default all teacher resources have the option to add portfolio pages.

You can add to a portfolio:

  • Pages that you have authored.
  • Pages by other authors for whom you have permission to edit their pages.

Other special portfolio features:

  • You can create sections for the portfolio pages you are adding. For example, if you are creating a portfolio called the Insects of San Antonio, you can create sections to organize pages by different types of insects or sections to organize pages by different habitats.
  • You can choose to display some of the data sections of the portfolio you create on the pages that you include in the portfolio. The data sections that you can display on pages added to the portfolio are References, Information on the Internet, and Learning Information. For example, when Rachel adds her Rachel's Backyard Bugs investigation treehouse to the portfolio she is creating called Insects of San Antonio, she can choose to display the References of Insects of San Antonio on Rachel's Backyard Bugs. Visitors to Rachel's Backyard Bugs will then see both the treehouse page's references as well as any references that were added to the portfolio Insects of San Antonio.
