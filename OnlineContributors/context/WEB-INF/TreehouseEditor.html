Step . Upload and/or Edit Media

In order to insert media files (images, sounds and movies) you create yourself or find on the WWW into your treehouse, you will first need to upload (add) them to the ToL database.

When you upload media to the ToL database you must add information, such as copyright, for each media file.

Step . Edit Learning Information

Add learning resource information like learner level and treehouse type so that others can easily find, visit and use your treehouse.

Step . Edit Support Materials

Add support materials such as worksheets, vocabulary words, instructions and more. Create web-based materials or upload documents from your computer.

 
edit this step
 

Step . Add Portfolio Pages

Add ToL pages that work together as a group of related information in order to create a teacher resource portfolio.

You can add ToL pages to your portfolio that you have authored or for which you have editing privileges. The pages you add here will be listed in a portfolio menu that is accessible from every page of the teacher resource portfolio.

 
edit this step
 

Step . Edit Attach to Group

To publish your treehouse you must attach it to the Branch or Leaf page for the group of organisms on the Tree of Life that your treehouse is about. For example, if your treehouse is about Ants, you would attach it to the Ant branch page. Visitors to the ToL will then be able to access your treehouse from the Ants branch page, and from ToL searches for Ants.

 
edit this step
 

Step . Add Notes, Questions and Comments

Add notes for yourself and/or questions and comments for ToL Editors or authors. Notes and comments will not be published on your treehouse.

 
edit this step
 

Step . Publish Your Treehouse

When you are satisfied with your page you should submit it for publication on the ToL web site. As soon as your page has been approved for publication you will receive an email with its URL (web address). Visitors to the ToL will be able to access your treehouse via the group of organisms to which you attached your treehouse.

Save your work!

To save your work and preview your treehouse click Save and Preview at the bottom of each editing step's page.

Warning! You must click Save and Preview from each treehouse building step before you close the treehouse editor window or you will lose your work.

Your ability to edit will time-out in 1 hour of inactivity. Therefore, we recommend that you save your work when you step away from the computer.

You are editing an Other Teacher Resource Format treehouse.

You are editing a Lesson/Project/Activity treehouse.

This format is for creating a lesson plan with the information and materials needed to implement the resource. In addition you have several options for organizing and adding information. You can:

  • Create a separate section for students to view, the "Learner Section"
  • Add support materials such as worksheets
  • Group together ToL pages related to your lesson to form a teacher resource portfolio.

You are editing a WebQuest treehouse. This format is for creating an interactive web-based activity. The learners who will engage in the WebQuest are the audience of the WebQuest introduction, task, process, evaluation and conclusion. Some WebQuests can be embarked upon without teacher guidance, however the "teacher page" provides information that would help a teacher use the webquest with their classroom.
